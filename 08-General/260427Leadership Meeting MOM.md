## **1. Purpose of the Meeting**

The meeting was convened to:

- Review China business execution speed versus corporate enablement
- Update on digital transformation and system deployments
- Discuss budgeting outcomes and KPI rollout
- Review BD pipeline credibility and portfolio performance
- Address partner performance, payments, and legal positioning
- Align on governance, audit, and board communication discipline

---

## **2. Business Context & General Observations**

- China business development operates at **very high speed**, with rapid movement from opportunity to construction.
- Corporate functions (digital, finance, governance) are **catching up** to business velocity.
- Leadership acknowledged this gap and emphasized **support**, while reinforcing expectations on **discipline, accountability, and accuracy**.
- Teams were asked to accept short‑term consolidation pain in favor of long‑term operational transparency and control.

## **3. Digital Transformation & Systems Rollout**

### Updates Provided

- A **Digital Blueprint** has been established.
- Major systems progress:
    - Construction management tools deployed
    - Fund Management system launched (previously manual for ~15 years)
    - Budgeting system to go live **June**
    - Asset Management System (v1) to be launched **June**, covering:
        - Production tracking (water & power)
        - Daily/weekly shortages
        - Tariff‑based financial impact
- Asset Manager soft‑launched successfully in:
    - Uzbekistan
    - Three project companies in Saudi Arabia
- Rollout to China ongoing; challenges remain with data tagging and integration.

### Key Discussion Points

- China currently has **too many fragmented systems and platforms**.
- IT and cybersecurity platforms require **consolidation**.
- Need for a **single source of truth**, even if transition is painful.

### Direction

- IT KPIs should include **platform reduction targets** (e.g. 50 → 40 → 30).
- System rollout to China to continue with focus on data quality and standardization.

## **4. Budgeting Process & Financial Assumptions**

### Issues Raised

- Budgeting exercise was described as **highly challenging**, particularly assumptions related to:
    - Immediate income generation from **4.5 GW as of January 1**
    - Lack of alignment with construction, COD, and water readiness realities
- Net income targets were driven by assumptions **without sufficient operational validation**.
- Similar issues experienced previously in Saudi Arabia, now acknowledged by leadership.

### Guidance from Leadership

- China teams should **not pre‑emptively reduce budgets**.
- Focus on **actual cash generation and delivery**, not defensive budget negotiations.
- Any budget line must be **defensible as essential**, anticipating potential cost pressure.
- Giving up budget early implies it was never needed.

---

## **5. KPI Rollout & Performance Framework**

### Decisions Confirmed

- KPI framework has been **approved by BEC and Board** and will not be changed.
- Only **one KPI family applies: “China Family”**.
- All functions (finance, operations, safety, cybersecurity, IT, BD) are measured against China KPIs collectively.
- Seniority increases weight of organizational performance in:
    - Appraisal
    - Bonus
    - Salary progression

### Concerns Highlighted

- Rollout timing is late (May–June), limiting adjustment period for staff.
- Safety and cybersecurity KPIs need to be **China‑specific**, focusing on:
    - Capability building
    - Standards rollout
    - Maturity progression rather than pure outcome metrics

## **10. Governance, Legal Process & Audit Discipline**

### Audit

- Zero tolerance for extensions.
- Audit items to be closed **ahead of deadlines**, not at deadline.
- Admin audits prioritized as baseline governance control.

### Legal & Documentation

- Introduce **tiered legal review**:
    - Full legal review for JV, AoA, binding contracts
    - Limited review for MOUs where appropriate
- Business document initiator accountable for:
    - Correct template usage
    - Identifying deviation and approval paths
- Stamping and approval processes to be unified.

---

## **11. Board / BEC Communication Discipline**

- **What is submitted = what must be presented**.
- Any update requires:
    - Version control
    - Clear change log
    - Explicit disclosure to the Board
- Loss of trust noted due to prior inconsistencies.
- Preferred approach:
    - Present approved content
    - Verbally flag updates for later circulation
