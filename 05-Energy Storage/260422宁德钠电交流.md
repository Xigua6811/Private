## Brief（会议概述）

  

本次会议围绕钠离子电池（Sodium-ion Battery, SIB）在储能领域的应用前景及与ACWA的潜在合作展开。双方从技术特性、系统集成方式、经济性拐点以及实际项目应用等多个维度进行了交流。

  

ACWA当前整体态度仍然偏谨慎，更关注在现有项目中技术的经济性与成熟度，而非前沿技术的提前导入。会议更多定位在“技术验证与节奏判断”，而不是短期内推进商业落地。

## 电力市场背景与需求变化

  

会议前讨论到国内电力市场的变化，尤其是中国西北地区已经开始实施**15分钟超短期实时电价机制**，并逐步走向自由交易。

  

这一变化对储能系统提出了新的要求：

- 调度响应需要更快、更灵活  

- SOC精度直接影响交易收益  

- 储能从“容量型资产”转向“交易型资产”  

  

在这个背景下，电池技术本身的“可控性”和“可预测性”变得越来越重要。

  

---

  

## 钠离子电池技术特性

  

钠电池与磷酸铁锂相比，在多个维度表现出差异化特点。

  

在低温性能方面，钠电池优势非常明显。在-20℃条件下，能量保持率可以达到92%以上，而LFP通常在60%左右，这使其在高寒地区具备天然优势。

  

在高温环境下，钠电池同样表现稳定，在约45℃条件下循环寿命可以达到13000次以上，同时由于热稳定性较好，系统层面对空调等热管理的依赖也相对降低。

  

另一个重要特点是SOC估算能力。由于钠电池的电压曲线具有明显斜率（不像LFP那样有长平台区），SOC可以通过电压更精确地反推，不需要频繁进行满充满放校准。这一点对于参与电力现货市场、进行高频调度具有实际价值。

  

在能量密度方面，当前钠电池仍然明显低于锂电：

- 当前约110–130 Wh/kg  

- 未来目标约160 Wh/kg  

- 对比LFP的180–200 Wh/kg仍有差距  

  

因此其应用需要依赖系统层设计进行补偿。

  

---

  

## 技术路线与材料体系

  

目前钠离子电池主要有几类技术路线：

  

聚阴离子体系（如 PO₄ / P₂O₇ / SO₄ / CO₃），容量在100–120 mAh/g之间，特点是成熟度较高，预计会最先大规模落地。

  

普鲁士白体系，容量可达到约160 mAh/g，属于较高能量密度方向。

  

层状氧钒化合物在110–160 mAh/g区间内探索。

  

整体来看，技术路线仍在演进过程中，尚未完全收敛。

  

---

  

## 系统结构与产品形态

  

由于钠电池能量密度偏低，其系统设计更加依赖结构优化。

  

宁德时代已推出“集装箱堆叠”方案，即通过**集装箱落集装箱的堆叠结构**来提升单位占地的能量密度。目前已有：

- 4.5 + 4.5 MWh 堆叠集装箱方案  

- 在南美已有约9 MWh级项目落地  

  

在产品规格方面：

- 单集装箱功率约2.3 MW  

- 小规模测试建议范围在100–1000 kWh  

- 100 kWh大致对应若干pack级组合  

  

需要特别注意的是，钠电池与锂电池**不能混装使用**，原因在于两者BMS体系完全不同。

  

---

  

## 商业化进展

  

钠离子电池目前已经在动力电池领域实现小批量上车，说明其基础技术已具备一定成熟度。

  

在储能领域，宁德时代预计在7月推出首款集装箱级产品样品，这将是其进入储能市场的重要节点。

  

整体来看，钠电仍处于从“技术验证”向“初步商业化”过渡的阶段。

## Brief

  

This meeting focused on the application prospects of **Sodium-ion Batteries (SIB)** in energy storage and potential collaboration with ACWA. Discussions covered multiple aspects including technical characteristics, system integration approaches, economic inflection points, and real project applications.

  

ACWA’s current stance remains relatively cautious, placing greater emphasis on **economic viability and technology maturity** in existing projects rather than early adoption of emerging technologies. The meeting was positioned more as **technology validation and timing assessment**, rather than immediate commercial deployment.

  

---

  

## Power Market Background and Changing Requirements

  

The discussion began with recent developments in China’s power market, particularly in Northwest regions where a **15-minute ultra-short-term real-time pricing mechanism** has been introduced, moving gradually toward a fully liberalized market.

  

This shift imposes new requirements on energy storage systems:

- Faster and more flexible dispatch response  

- SOC accuracy directly impacting trading revenue  

- Transition of storage from **capacity-based assets to trading-oriented assets**  

  

In this context, **controllability and predictability of battery technologies** are becoming increasingly critical.

  

---

  

## Technical Characteristics of Sodium-ion Batteries

  

Compared with LFP batteries, sodium-ion batteries demonstrate distinct advantages:

  

- **Low-temperature performance:**  

  At -20°C, energy retention can exceed 92%, compared to ~60% for LFP, making SIB highly suitable for cold climates.

  

- **High-temperature performance:**  

  Stable operation at ~45°C with cycle life exceeding 13,000 cycles. Improved thermal stability reduces reliance on HVAC systems.

  

- **SOC estimation capability:**  

  Due to the sloped voltage curve (unlike the flat plateau of LFP), SOC can be inferred more accurately from voltage, reducing the need for frequent full charge/discharge calibration. This is particularly valuable for high-frequency dispatch in electricity markets.

  

- **Energy density limitations:**  

  - Current: ~110–130 Wh/kg  

  - Target: ~160 Wh/kg  

  - Still lower than LFP (~180–200 Wh/kg)  

  

As a result, system-level design is required to compensate for lower energy density.

  

---

  

## Technology Routes and Material Systems

  

Current sodium-ion battery technologies include:

  

- **Polyanion systems** (e.g., PO₄ / P₂O₇ / SO₄ / CO₃)  

  - Capacity: 100–120 mAh/g  

  - Most mature and likely to scale first  

  

- **Prussian White systems**  

  - Capacity up to ~160 mAh/g  

  - Higher energy density direction  

  

- **Layered oxide systems**  

  - Capacity range: 110–160 mAh/g  

  

Overall, the technology landscape is still evolving and has not yet converged.

  

---

  

## System Architecture and Product Form

  

Due to lower energy density, sodium-ion systems rely heavily on structural optimization.

  

CATL has introduced a **stacked container solution**, where containers are vertically stacked to increase energy density per land area:

  

- 4.5 + 4.5 MWh stacked container configuration  

- ~9 MWh projects already deployed in South America  

  

Product specifications:

- Single container power: ~2.3 MW  

- Recommended pilot scale: 100–1000 kWh  

- ~100 kWh corresponds to pack-level configurations  

  

**Important:** Sodium-ion and lithium-ion batteries **cannot be mixed**, due to fundamentally different BMS systems.

  

---

  

## Commercialization Progress

  

Sodium-ion batteries have already achieved **small-scale deployment in EV applications**, indicating baseline technological maturity.

  

For energy storage:

- CATL plans to release its **first containerized SIB product in July**  

  

Overall, SIB is transitioning from **technology validation to early-stage commercialization**.

  

---

  

## Demonstration Platforms and Testing Capabilities

  

CATL is building a comprehensive energy storage demonstration platform in Xiamen:

  

- Scale: ~50 MW (grid-level)  

- Construction: expected completion in Q2–Q3  

- External access: expected in Q3 (subject to delay)  

  

The core laboratory is located in Ningde, with potential supporting facilities in Shanghai (to be confirmed).

  

This platform could support **joint testing with clients** and represents a key collaboration opportunity.

  

---

  

## ACWA Internal Strategy

  

The Christal team previously considered deploying sodium-ion batteries in a **125 MW project (March 2026)**. However, ACWA management concluded:

  

- Priority should remain on **economic performance and technology maturity**  

- Avoid premature adoption of emerging technologies  

  

Current strategy:

- Continuous tracking  

- No early large-scale deployment  

- Rapid entry when conditions are favorable  

  

---

  

## Testing Platform and Collaboration Direction

  

ACWA plans to establish a **small-scale sodium-ion battery testing platform in Shanghai**, with a capacity of 100–1000 kWh.

  

Objectives include:

- Technical performance validation  

- Supporting global R&D (Christal team) for technology selection  

- Providing data for future projects  

  

CATL’s potential contributions:

- Supply of product samples (to be confirmed)  

- Access to Xiamen demonstration platform  

  

Both parties agreed that testing should:

- Be conducted under NDA  

- Ensure transparency and data verifiability  

  

---

  

## Key Conclusions

  

No immediate commercial decisions were made.

  

However, both parties aligned on:

- Initiating small-scale testing  

- Continuously monitoring technology development  

- Focusing on cost and performance inflection points over the next 2–3 years  

  

Sodium-ion batteries are positioned as a **potential alternative pathway**, rather than a near-term mainstream solution.
